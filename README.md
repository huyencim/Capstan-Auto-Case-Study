# Capstan Autos Case Study

## Overview
This repository contains the solution to the *Capstan Autos* case study, focusing on financial analysis, short-term planning, and strategic financing at an East Coast automobile dealership.

## Objectives
- Assess Capstan Autos’s financial condition
- Evaluate if the company is in financial trouble
- Determine if the bank’s credit restriction is justified
- Explain rising indebtedness despite higher profits
- Provide actionable financial recommendations

## Case Summary
- Imported and sold vehicles quarterly, paying the manufacturer at quarter-end
- Introduced a six-month free credit policy, boosting sales but tying up cash in accounts receivable
- Debt increased to ~\$9.7M, making the bank hesitant to extend further credit

## Methodology
- Financial ratio analysis: debt ratio, equity ratio, current ratio
- Trend analysis: profits vs. debt accumulation
- Cash flow evaluation: effect of credit policy
- Credit assessment: justification of bank decisions

## Key Insights
- Debt rose due to extended customer credit
- High leverage reduced financial flexibility
- Bank’s credit restriction is justified
- Profits mask cash flow shortages

## Repository Contents
- Financial worksheets (`.xlsx`)
- Narrative answers and analysis
- Recommendations

## Usage
1. Open the solution file to view analysis
2. Review financial worksheets included
3. Reference conclusions and recommendations for submission or discussion

## Notes
- Results are based on case-provided data
- Cash flow analysis is central to understanding short-term financial stress
- Assumes consistent accounting practices across periods
